A Mouthful of Thorns

Etching, 2024

“The princess who refused to wake. Not because she could not. Because she had seen what waking means.”

The etching process is a kind of negotiation with the metal — you scratch, you bite with acid, you ink, you wipe, you press, and what emerges is never quite what you intended. This piece took that negotiation as its subject.

The sleeping figure is based on the Briar Rose variants of Sleeping Beauty, but the thorns grow from inside — from the mouth, the throat, the lungs. She is not trapped by the castle's hedge. She is the hedge. The curse and the cure have become the same thing.

I worked on zinc plate, using hard ground for the fine lines of the thorns and soft ground for the texture of the fabric and skin. The print was pulled on Japanese hosho paper, the kind that absorbs ink like a sponge, giving the blacks a velvet depth that digital reproduction can only hint at.

This is the piece that collectors ask about most often. I think it is because they recognize something in her stillness — a refusal that is also an acceptance, a mouthful of thorns that is also a prayer.
