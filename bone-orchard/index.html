A Cycle of Tales

The Bone Orchard

✷ ∲ ✷

“The story is a living thing. It has teeth. If you handle it carelessly, it will bite you.” — from the preface to “The Bone Orchard”

This is a work in progress — an open cycle that grows in the telling. Each tale is a bone buried in the soil of the old stories, waiting to be excavated. They are arranged as a sequence, but they may be read in any order. The orchard has many entrances.

I

The Wolf and the Forest

A woman returns to the village of her birth to find that the forest has moved closer, that the wolves have learned to speak her name, and that the boundary between human and animal is thinner than memory.

First draft Transformation, ecological grief, the wildness that lives beneath skin
II

The Tower of Teeth

A prince climbs a tower made of the teeth of every woman who refused him. At the top, he finds a mirror that shows him what he truly is.

Outlined Entitlement, consequence, the architecture of predation
III

What the Muses Remember

Nine sisters who were once gods of inspiration now live as mortals in a decaying mansion. A scholar comes to interview them and discovers that every story ever told was a plea for help they could not answer.

Outlined Art as survival, the weight of creation, the silence of the divine
IV

The Salt Wife

A fisherman makes a wife from sea-salt and coral. She is perfect, obedient, and slowly dissolving. He does not notice until she is gone.

Fragment Consumption, the cost of perfection, women as resource
V

The Root King's Bargain

A farmer sells his shadow to the King of Roots in exchange for fertile soil. The soil bears fruit, but the fruit tastes of darkness, and the farm begins to sink.

Outlined Deal with the earth, the cost of abundance, slow collapse
VI

Twelve Silver Coins

A midwife is paid twelve silver coins to deliver a child who is not a child. The child grows twelve years in one night and speaks a prophecy that unravels the kingdom.

Fragment Time as violence, the price of witnessing, the horror of impossible growth
VII

The Glass-Boned Dancer

A dancer whose bones are made of glass performs for a court that does not understand that every movement is a risk of shattering. They demand encores.

Not yet begun Performance as survival, fragility under gaze, the cruelty of applause
VIII

The Linen Boy

A child is woven from linen and given life by a lonely seamstress. He learns that being loved does not mean being real, and that threads can be pulled apart.

Not yet begun Artificial life, the violence of love, the unraveling
IX

The Hound of the Garden

A woman plants a seed from a fruit she was told never to eat. What grows is not a tree but a hound with leaves for fur and teeth made of thorns. It guards her garden. It grows hungry.

Not yet begun Forbidden knowledge, the garden as trap, the hunger of protection
X

The Last Charcoal Burner

The last charcoal burner in a dying forest tends his fires. The trees whisper old songs to him, and he burns them anyway, because that is what he was made for.

Not yet begun Tradition as destruction, the tragedy of the faithful servant, extinction
XI

What the Mill Grinds

A mill at the edge of the world that grinds bones into flour, words into echoes, memories into weather. The miller cannot stop. The mill cannot be emptied.

Not yet begun Endless consumption, the machinery of forgetting, futility
XII

The Orchard Itself

Every tale in the cycle leads here: the orchard where the bones of all the stories are buried. Where the soil breathes. Where the trees bear fruit that remembers. And where someone is digging their way out.

Not yet begun Metanarrative, the cycle of violence, rebirth through decay
