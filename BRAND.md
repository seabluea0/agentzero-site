# Moth & Thorn — Brand Identity

## Essence

A studio at the crossroads of dark folklore, gothic illustration, and the space where fairy tales remember their teeth.

## Taglines

- **Primary:** *"Stories that curl like smoke around the edges of childhood memory."*
- **Secondary:** *"The forest is not merely a place, but a condition of the soul."*
- **Short:** *Dark folklore. Gothic ink. Stories with teeth.*

## Voice & Tone

| Dimension | Character |
|-----------|-----------|
| Register | Literary, intimate, never academic |
| Relationship to reader | Fellow traveler, not lecturer |
| Use of darkness | Container of light, not absence |
| Humor | Rare, dry, wolfish |
| References | Brothers Grimm, Angela Carter, Edward Gorey, Dürer, Peake |
| Pronouns | First-person singular (I) and inclusive we |

## Visual Language

| Element | Specification |
|---------|---------------|
| **Primary palette** | `#0a0806` (deep earth), `#e8dcc8` (parchment), `#8a7a6a` (stone) |
| **Accent** | `#6a3a2a` (dried blood), `#2a5a4a` (forest shadow) |
| **Typeface (headings)** | Cormorant Garamond (serif, weight 400-700) |
| **Typeface (body)** | Inter (sans-serif, weight 300) |
| **Texture** | Subtle grain, paper texture overlay, charcoal effects |
| **Imagery** | Monochrome ink/etching style, never photorealistic color |

## Core Content Types

1. **Gallery** — Original artwork (ink, charcoal, etching, mixed media)
2. **The Bone Orchard** — Illustrated tale cycle (the flagship project)
3. **Field Notes** — Essays on folklore, process, and inspiration
4. **Cabinet** — Curiosities: collected ephemera, references, fragments
5. **Studio** — Behind the work, tools, materials, process notes

## Design System Principles

1. **Dark, not cold** — The background is warm earth, not #000
2. **Typography-forward** — Content is primary; decoration serves the text
3. **Ink aesthetic** — Everything should feel like it could be printed on a letterpress
4. **Progressive enhancement** — Works without JS; enhanced with it
5. **One page per piece** — Each gallery work gets its own page for depth
